PROJECT DETAILS

GENERAL PRINCIPLES OF PREVENTION

Schedule 1 - Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999:

  • 1. Avoiding risks: Avoid risks altogether where possible
  • 2. Evaluating unavoidable risks: Evaluate risks that cannot be avoided
  • 3. Combating risks at source: Combat risks at their source rather than taking palliative measures
  • 4. Adapting work to the individual: In particular, as regards the design of workplaces, the choice of work equipment and working and production methods, with a view to alleviating monotonous work and work at a predetermined work-rate and to reducing their effect on health
  • 5. Adapting to technical progress: Take advantage of technical progress
  • 6. Replacing dangerous with harmless: Replace the dangerous by the non-dangerous or the less dangerous
  • 7. Developing coherent prevention policy: Develop a coherent overall prevention policy which covers technology, organisation of work, working conditions, social relationships and the influence of factors relating to the working environment
  • 8. Giving collective protective measures priority: Give collective protective measures priority over individual protective measures
  • 9. Giving appropriate instructions: Give appropriate instructions to employees

Note: These principles must be applied by designers when designing structures under CDM 2015 Regulation 9 (2).
